The conservation of large, long-lived turtle species can be a challenging issue because their life-history strategies make populations sensitive to changes in adult survivorship and populations may be difficult to sample. The Alligator Snapping Turtle ( Macrochelys temminckii ) is a large freshwater turtle species occurring in North America for which commercial harvest has severely reduced populations throughout the species range.
