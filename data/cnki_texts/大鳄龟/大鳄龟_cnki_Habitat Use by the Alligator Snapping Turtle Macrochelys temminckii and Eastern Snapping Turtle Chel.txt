This study addresses habitat differences of two sympatric turtle species, alligator snapping turtles ( Macrochelys temminckii ) and eastern snapping turtles ( Chelydra serpentina ) in six watersheds in southeastern Missouri. We found that alligator snapping turtle presence corresponded with higher abundance of submerged physical structures in the stream, deeper water, relatively higher levels of detritus, and warmer water temperatures.
