The internal organs of chelonians are exposed to intra-coelomic pressure during limb movement and breathing due to the presence of the shell. Pressure on organs alters the hemodynamics and has functional implications. The chelonian liver occupies most of the cranial portion of the coelomic cavity and can be susceptible to this pressure.
